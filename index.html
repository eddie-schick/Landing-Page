Manual, Paper-Driven Processes

Dealers are operating with archaic, paper-driven processes for vehicle configuration, quoting, coordination, and delivery that involve 40+ manual steps per sale

Excessive Software Costs

Dealers waste $200k-$500k annually juggling disconnected software systems (CRM, DMS, inventory management, etc.) that don't integrate with each other

Costly Human Errors

Manual processes lead to frequent documentation errors causing delays, rework, and costly mistakes like incorrect order forms that result in expensive change fees and lost opportunities

Time-Consuming Administrative Tasks

Sales staff lose 5-8 hours per week on administrative tasks, with employees spending hours weekly on manual data entry and admin time on data reconciliation

Poor Multi-Stakeholder Coordination

Dealers must manage manual touchpoints with multiple partners (OEMs, upfitters, logistics companies, body manufacturers) with no centralized system, leading to constant back-and-forth calls and emails

Lack of Real-Time Visibility

Dealers face "where's my truck?" customer inquiry daily due to scattered status information across multiple OEM portals, upfitter updates, and logistics systems with no single source of truth

Document Management Chaos

Physical document storage and scattered digital files lead to 30-45 minute average search times, lost documents, no audit trails, and weeks of audit preparation time

Inadequate Existing Solutions

Current CRM systems lack the sophistication and customization needed for the commercial vehicle sales lifecycle, while comprehensive DMS systems are too costly for small to mid-sized dealers
